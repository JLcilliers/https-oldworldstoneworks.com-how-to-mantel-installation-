How to Install a Stone Fireplace Mantel

At a Glance
What to Know Before Installing Your Cast Stone Mantel

Installing a cast stone mantel requires careful preparation, the right mortar mix, and ideally a professional mason. Mantels arrive as separate stones that are anchored to the wall and mortared together on-site.

Use a standard mortar mix, keeping it 1" from the surface, then finish with the white mortar mix type N provided. Protect joint edges with blue painter's tape before grouting, and remove within 24–48 hours.

After installation, clean with a 1:7 muriatic acid solution, rinse thoroughly, and seal once fully dry. Allow lower sections to fully set before adding heavy upper elements.

Cast Stone Mantel Installation Overview

Installation should be done by a professional mason. Cast stone mantels are heavy and often require two or more people to lift certain pieces. Safety is paramount — handle with care to avoid personal injury or damage.

Note that while we suggest one method of installation, there are several acceptable techniques. Old World Stoneworks is not responsible for installation outcomes. This full-length installation video can be used by homeowners, masons, and other installers.

How to Install a Mantel on a Stone Fireplace

Step 1 – Unpacking & Layout

Unpacking is crucial. Carefully unpack each stone, avoiding damage to edges and corners. Lay out all pieces on a protective surface such as styrofoam, cardboard, or plywood. This helps verify that all pieces are undamaged and measurements are accurate.

Ensure there is a ¼" space between each stone during layout. Handle edges delicately to prevent damage. Ensure you check the packing information for the weight of each stone.

Step 2 – Mortar Mix & Application

Use a standard mortar mix for installation, keeping the mortar 1" away from the surface, then finish with pointing up using the sand and white mortar mix type N provided.

If white mortar mix type N is unavailable, a mixture of 3 parts sand, 1 part lime, and 1 part white Portland cement can be used. After the grout begins to set, complete any final pointing up, then remove the masking tape.

For mantels with heavy upper parts, allow lower sections to set before adding weighty top elements.

Step 3 – Grouting & Joint Protection

Before grouting, protect the joint edges with masking tape or consider using water as an alternative method. Since stone is porous, unprotected edges can allow lime to leach from the joints, resulting in a fuzzy appearance.

We recommend using blue painter's tape for best results. Ensure the stone surface is dry and clean for optimal tape adherence. The tape should not remain on for more than 24–48 hours.

Step 4 – Cleaning After Installation

After installation, clean your cast stone mantel using a mixture of muriatic acid and water (1 part acid to 7 parts water). Ensure the grout is dry and protect nearby surfaces with plastic drop cloths.

Start by misting the mantel with water, then spray the acid solution from top to bottom. Lightly scrub with a nylon brush for even cleaning, and rinse thoroughly with water after 30 seconds to a minute to avoid etching.

Repeat as necessary for stubborn stains, and allow the mantel to dry completely before sealing.

Safety & Important Notes

  • Installation should always be completed by a professional mason.
  • Cast stone mantels are heavy — two or more people are required to lift certain pieces.
  • Handle all edges delicately to prevent chipping or damage.
  • Old World Stoneworks is not responsible for installation outcomes.
  • Always verify all pieces are present and undamaged before beginning installation.
